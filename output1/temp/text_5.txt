## What are the Government Schemes for Farmers

Government schemes for farmers are basically government approved financial schemes, initiatives, programs, and policies to provide support, assistance, and resources to individuals and communities engaged in agricultural sections. These government provided schemes are designed to handle the natural challenges (draught, irrigation, soil degradation) faced by the farmers, improve agricultural productivity, ensure food security, promote sustainable techniques and develop rural livelihoods. The government schemes for farmers in india will cover financial aid, technological advancements, market access, insurance, and infrastructure development, among other aspects. The extensive goal of government subsidy schemes for farmers is invented to empower farmers, strengthen the agricultural sector, and pay impact to GDP in India.

## Top 10 Government Schemes for Farmers in India

### Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN) is very significant agricultural welfare scheme initiated by the Government of India to provide direct income support to small and marginal farmers across the country.

![PM Kisan Samman Nidhi Scheme 1](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/PM-Kisan-Samman-Nidhi-Scheme-1-1024x597.jpg)**Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi**(Picture Source: Divya Sandesh)

#### **Benefits:**

  * Direct income support of ₹6,000 per year.
  * Financial aid is provided in three equal installments.
  * To increase farmers income and livelihoods.
  * Provide the financial aid to purchase seeds, fertilizers, and machines.
  * Alleviates financial stress on small and marginal farmers.
  * Improves the agricultural productivity and yields.
  * Supports the rural economic growth and development.
  * Reduces dependency on informal credit sources.
  * Helps in farm planning and management of farming activities.



#### **Target Group:**

The scheme primarily targets small and marginal farmers, constituting very substantial portion of India’s agricultural community, aiming to help their financial burden and enhance agricultural productivity.

**official website** – https://pmkisan.gov.in/

### Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) 

The Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY) – Government Schemes for farmers, is the flagship agricultural insurance scheme started by the Indian government to provide financial protection against crop yield losses caused by unforeseen events.

![c3ac2bfa d9d4 11eb a5ce a68058c7c394 1625079441804](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/c3ac2bfa-d9d4-11eb-a5ce-a68058c7c394_1625079441804-1024x576.webp)**Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana**(Picture Source: Hindustan Times)

#### **Benefits:**

  * Provides affordable crop insurance to farmers.
  * Covers yield losses due to natural calamities, pests, and diseases.
  * Reduces the financial burden on farmers during crop failures.
  * Reduce the risk in the field and provide stability in the agriculture sector.
  * Ensures on time compensation for eligible farmers.
  * Helps to adopt of modern technology for agricultural cultivation.
  * Helps the farmers to spend money in agricultural activities.



**official website** – https://pmfby.gov.in/

#### **Target Group:**

The PMFBY scheme is designed to benefit to the all small, marginal, and large landholders across the nation to enhance their lives and promotes agricultural sustainability.

### The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme 

The Kisan Credit Card (KCC) Scheme is an agricultural credit scheme introduced by the Government of India in 1998 to provide the suitable and real-time access to money for their farming requirements and other agricultural expenses.

![Kisan Credit Card Apply Online Kisan Loan Scheme jpg](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/Kisan-Credit-Card-Apply-Online-Kisan-Loan-Scheme-jpg-1024x577.webp)**The Kisan Credit Card Scheme** (Picture Source: Himalayan Express)

#### **Benefits:**

  * Offers short-term credit for agricultural and allied activities.
  * Boosts the farmers to purchase inputs like seeds, fertilizers, and machinery.
  * Covers expenses related to crop cultivation, post-harvest handling, and marketing.
  * Reduces the dependence on informal credit sources and moneylenders.
  * Improves the financial inclusion by extending credit to farmers who will not have traditional banking access.
  * Improvement of the farm productivity, crops yield, and gross income.



**official website** – https://www.myscheme.gov.in/schemes/kcc

#### **Target Group:**

The Kisan Credit Card scheme is aimed at all small and marginal farmers across the country to address their credit needs efficiently and promote rural economic development.

### Soil Health Card Scheme 

The Soil Health Card Scheme is the agricultural Government schemes for farmers in India which pay attention on estimating and improving the soil health of farmers’ fields, desiring to enhance crop productivity and sustainability.

![Presentation1 3](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/Presentation1-3-1024x576.jpg)**Soil Health Card Scheme**

#### **Benefits:**

  * Provides farmers soil health information of their corresponding field.
  * Give the recommendations to use balanced fertilizers with optimum quantity.
  * Enriches agricultural productivity and profit.
  * Boosts the sustainable and eco-friendly farming practices.
  * Reduces nutrient imbalances and soil degradation from soil.
  * Supports in time of decision-making for farmers.
  * Contributes to the conservation of natural resources.



#### **Target Group:**

The Soil Health Card Goverment Schemes for farmers targets all farmers – small and marginal- across the nation in the time of improving soil quality.

### National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) 

The National Agriculture Market (e-NAM) is the life changing initiative desired at modernizing and facilitating the agricultural trading system in India. It jump on the digital technology to create the harmonious national market for agricultural commodities, enhancing transparency and efficiency in the history of Indian economy.

![market 2](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/market-2-1024x576.jpg)**National Agriculture Market** | **Government Schemes for Farmer in India**

#### **Benefits:**

  * Facilitates price discovery and market broadcasting process.
  * Enhances price completion for farmers by providing access to the larger market.
  * Promoting direct farmer-buyer interactions.
  * Improves agricultural supply chain, optimizes wastage management, and ensures on time deliveries.
  * Expands the market which is accessible to the small and marginal farmers to increase their consumer reach.
  * Maintains consistency in agro product standards through grading and quality certification.
  * Encouraged to take perticipation in the competition among buyers, resulting in better prices for farmers.
  * Empowers farmers with greater control over selling decisions and income potential.



**official website** – https://www.enam.gov.in/web/

#### **Target Group:**

Farmers, traders, agricultural marketing committees, state governments, and buyers constitute the primary beneficiaries and participants of the e-NAM platform.

### Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) 

Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY) is an Indian government initiative that promotes organic farming and sustainable agricultural practices. It works in the zone which is related to improving soil health, increasing farmer income, and ensuring environmental sustainability.

![pkvy pics](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/pkvy-pics-1024x321.jpg)**Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana**{Picture Source: Seekhe)

#### **Benefits:**

  * Enhances soil fertility and health.
  * Reduces dependency on chemical inputs.
  * Increases farmer income through organic premium.
  * Supports eco-friendly agricultural practices.
  * Improves the water conservation.



#### **Target Group:**

Farmers across India, especially those practicing traditional agriculture, are the primary beneficiaries of the PKVY scheme.

### Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) 

Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana (PMKSY) is the flagship program launched by the Government of India with the purpose of expanding irrigation coverage, improving water-use efficiency, and promoting sustainable agricultural practices.

![Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/Pradhan-Mantri-Krishi-Sinchai-Yojana-1024x576.png)**Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai** **Yojana**(Picture Source: Indian Bureaucracy)

#### **Benefits:**

  * Enhances agricultural productivity by providing reliable water supply.
  * Improves optimize water utilization through modern irrigation techniques.
  * Facilitates in water management and conservation through sustainable agriculture.
  * Addresses the local differences in irrigation infrastructure.



**official website** – https://pmksy.gov.in/microirrigation/index.aspx

#### **Target Group:**

Farmers, agricultural communities, and water resource management agencies across India are the primary beneficiaries of the Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchai Yojana.

### **R** ashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) 

The Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY) is Goverment schemes for farmer launched in India to support and promote agricultural development and enhance the overall well-being of farmers in all the states and union territories.

![Government Schemes for Farmers](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/nadp-1024x576.jpg)**Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana** | Government Schemes for Farmers

#### **Benefits:**

  * Provides financial assistance for various agriculture-related projects and activities.
  * Helps in time of implementation of modern agronomical technology and trendy agricultural practices.
  * Pursues to increase agricultural productivity and farmer income.
  * Supports in sustainable rural development initiatives.
  * Build the design of agricultural infrastructure.



**official website** – https://rkvy.nic.in/

#### **Target Group:**

Farmers, agricultural entrepreneurs, state and union territory governments, and organizations involved in agricultural development are the primary beneficiaries and participants of the Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana.

### PM-Kisan Pension Yojana 

The Pradhan Mantri Kisan Pension Yojana (PM-Kisan Pension Yojana) is the social security initiative by the Government of India to provide monthly basis regular pensions to small and marginal farmers. The purpose is – to ensure the financial stability and welfare during their old age.

![pptB8B4.pptm Autosaved 1](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/pptB8B4.pptm-Autosaved-1-1024x576.jpg)**PM-Kisan Pension Yojana** | Government Schemes for Farmer

#### **Benefits:**

  * Offers a sustainable source of income to eligible farmers after retirement.
  * Provides financial aid to the farmers to reduce susceptibilities in old age.
  * Support in rural development by addressing various economical issues among the farmers.



**official website** – https://maandhan.in/

#### **Target Group:**

Small and marginal farmers across India are the primary beneficiaries of the PM-Kisan Pension Yojana. It strives to alleviate the financial hardships faced by these farmers during their retirement years.

### Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS)

The Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS) is the key scheme launched by the Government of India to promote agripreneurship in the dairy sector. It is designed to empower dairy farmers, increase the milk production, and generate self-employment.

![dairy entrepreneurship development scheme](https://timesofagriculture.in/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/dairy-entrepreneurship-development-scheme-1024x576.jpg)**Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme**(Picture Source: Go Digit)

#### **Benefits:**

  * Facilitates the establishment of modern dairy infrastructure and facilities.
  * Promotes value addition to milk through processing and product diversification.
  * Encourages entrepreneurship and self-employment in the dairy sector.
  * Enhances milk production, quality, and overall productivity.
  * Contributes to rural livelihoods and economic development.



#### **Target Group:**

Aspiring dairy entrepreneurs, individual farmers, self-help groups, and dairy cooperatives are the primary beneficiaries of the Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme. The aspiration of the government schemes for farmers is – to support individuals and groups interested in venturing into the dairy business by providing financial assistance and technical guidance.
